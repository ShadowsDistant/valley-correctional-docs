## Operational Mandates

### Universal Managerial Responsibilities

1. **Standard-Bearer** — Embody the highest level of professionalism and rule adherence.
2. **Strategic Alignment** — Coordinate with fellow managers so changes in one division don't negatively impact the broader facility.
3. **Community Engagement** — Maintain a consistent presence within the facility to gauge the pulse of membership and identify emerging issues.

### Division-Specific Directives

:::details Community Manager
- Direct mentorship and performance appraisal of the Management tier.
- Final arbiter for inter-divisional disputes and high-level strategy.
- Verification of operational integrity across all sectors.
:::

:::details Prison Administration (DoC)
- Primary liaison for Department of Corrections operations.
- Oversight of shift scheduling, host quality control, and server management.
- End-to-end management of the DoC recruitment and training pipeline.
:::

:::details Internal Operations (MOD + SID)
- Upholding impartiality and investigative integrity across SID.
- Equitable rule enforcement across Moderation.
- Strict enforcement of confidentiality and evidence protocols.
- Moderator and investigator roster management.
:::

:::details Development
- Development lifecycle and project deadline management.
- Onboarding and technical task allocation.
- Translating community requirements into technical specifications.
:::

## Governing Regulatory Policies

:::info Policy adherence
Failure to comply with the following policies will result in an **immediate SID referral**.
:::

### Authority & Jurisdiction

- **Executive Authority:** reserved for the **Community Manager** — allows staff-wide policy intervention and strategic overrides.
- **Scope of Authority:** managers operate exclusively within assigned divisions. Rank in one division does not confer authority in another (the Community Manager is exempt).
- **Command Authorization:** in-game and Discord moderation commands are restricted to **rostered Moderation personnel**. Managers not on the Moderation roster are prohibited from using these tools.

### Oversight Committee Approval

:::warning
Major policy changes, handbook amendments, and significant hiring decisions require formal review and approval from the **Oversight Committee** before they may be enacted. This applies to **every** manager, including the Community Manager.
:::

### Employment Blacklisting

Blacklisting is reserved for severe unprofessionalism or application fraud.

- **Issuance Authority:** blacklists may be issued by the **Community Manager** or the **Lead Overseer**.
- **Reason Confidentiality:** reasons are internal unless release is approved by **both** the Community Manager and the Lead Overseer.

1. **Access the Panel** — Navigate to the [Melonly Actions Panel](https://melonly.xyz/panel/7377150723760328704).
2. **Initiate Action** — Select **Actions → Employment Blacklist**.
3. **Documentation** — Provide detailed rationale and supporting evidence, then submit the form for logging.

:::note
All blacklist appeals route exclusively to the Community Manager.
:::

## Dismissal & Termination Protocols

Removal of a member from the Management Division is executed via four authorized channels:

1. **Peer Consensus** — Majority vote from the Management Division.
2. **Executive Action** — Community Manager dismissal for gross mismanagement.
3. **SID Finding** — Formal "Termination" recommendation following a misconduct investigation.
4. **Oversight Intervention** — Majority vote by the Oversight Committee and Lead Overseer approval.

:::note
The Community Manager may only be dismissed by a majority vote of the full Oversight Committee with Lead Overseer approval.
:::

### Management Vote of Removal (General Staff)

- **Scope:** any staff member outside Management, including divisional leadership.
- **Grounds:** gross negligence, severe code-of-conduct breach, or actions causing significant harm to community integrity.
- **Threshold:** simple majority (>50%) of active managers.
- **Appeal:** the subject is entitled to an **OSC Procedural Review** to verify due process was followed.

## At-Will Removal Authority

:::note Lead Overseer & Community Manager authority
Per the [General Staff Handbook](/internal-documents/general-staff-handbook), staff members may be removed **for any reason at any time** by the **Lead Overseer** (acting unilaterally) or the **Community Manager** (with Lead Overseer approval). Removals under this clause are final and not subject to the standard SID appeals process.
:::

## Community Manager — Special Rules

:::warning
The Community Manager occupies a unique position and is subject to enhanced procedural protections:

- **Investigation:** any SID investigation targeting the Community Manager **requires mandatory Lead Overseer involvement** at every stage. Any punishment must receive Lead Overseer approval before execution. See the [SID Handbook](/internal-documents/specialized-investigations-division-handbook).
- **Dismissal:** the Community Manager may only be dismissed by **majority vote of the full Oversight Committee** with **Lead Overseer approval**.
- **At-Will Removal Exemption:** the at-will removal authority above does **not** apply to the Community Manager.
:::

## Separation of Service

When a staff member leaves or is terminated:

1. **T+0 — Notification** — The managing authority notifies SID, the Oversight Committee, and the Development team.
2. **T+24 hours — Access Revocation** — All Discord roles, Melonly access, and administrative permissions must be fully revoked within 24 hours.
3. **T+7 days — Audit Log** — SID conducts a short audit of the departing member's final 30 days of activity to identify any outstanding compliance issues.

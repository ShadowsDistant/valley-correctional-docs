## Code of Conduct Violations

SID has jurisdiction over the following categories of staff misconduct.

:::details Command Abuse
Misuse of administrative commands or permissions beyond their intended purpose.
:::

:::details Failure to Moderate
Ignoring rule violations, or applying inconsistent or absent enforcement.
:::

:::details Biased Moderation
Allowing personal bias, friendships, or feelings to influence administrative decisions.
:::

:::details Failure to Uphold Standards
Falling short of the professional and procedural standards expected of staff.
:::

:::details Disclosure of Classified Information
Unauthorized sharing of confidential information. **Zero-tolerance offense.**
:::

:::details Mismanagement
Supervisory failures affecting operations, team morale, or community integrity.
:::

:::details General Staff Handbook Violations
Any breach of the baseline expectations set out in the [General Staff Handbook](/internal-documents/general-staff-handbook).
:::

:::details Terms of Service Violations
Platform ToS violations by staff — treated as a severe breach and escalated accordingly.
:::

## Disciplinary Point System

:::info Rolling expiration
Points accumulate on a **6-month rolling basis**. Older points expire once they pass the six-month window.
:::

**Points scale with severity.** Minor violations carry the fewest points; the most serious carry the most.

| Points | Tier | Examples |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | Minor | Inactivity, minor procedural lapses |
| **2–3** | Moderate | Repeated minor issues, moderate conduct violations |
| **4–5** | Serious | Serious conduct or enforcement failures |
| **6** | Severe | Disclosure of classified information, retaliation |

### Punishment Thresholds

| Accumulated Points | Action |
| :--- | :--- |
| **1 point** | Verbal warning |
| **3+ points** | 7-day suspension |
| **6+ points** | Termination |

### Mandatory-Termination Offenses

Certain offenses warrant immediate termination regardless of accumulated points:

- Confirmed disclosure of classified information (malicious).
- Retaliation against a whistleblower (Tier 3 offense).
- Serious Terms of Service violations (CSAM, real-world threats, ban evasion).
- Command abuse causing significant harm.
- Fraud in the application or investigation process.
- Direct threats to VCF operations.

## Standard Investigative Procedure

1. **Initiation** — A report is received via confidential SID ticket and assessed for jurisdiction and severity.
2. **Evidence Collection** — Investigators gather evidence, logs, and testimony while preserving chain of custody.
3. **Documentation** — Findings are recorded in a clear, objective case file.
4. **Conclusion** — A determination and recommended action are issued; escalated to the Oversight Committee where required.

## SID-Specific Policies

:::details ToS Violation Escalation Protocol
All confirmed ToS violations are logged and escalated so SID can maintain the cross-platform reporting record.

1. Confirm and document the violation with evidence.
2. Apply the immediate moderation action (ban/mute) if not already applied.
3. Escalate to SID for record-keeping and potential reporting to Discord Trust & Safety, Roblox moderation, or Valley Prison staff.
:::

:::details Lawful Directive Framework
Investigators execute lawful directives from supervisory staff, but must refuse and report directives that would violate policy or confidentiality.
:::

:::details Whistleblower Protection
Reporter identity is never shared with the subject of an investigation. Retaliation is a **Tier 3 offense** triggering immediate termination.
:::

:::details Peer Investigation Oversight
Investigations involving a peer or a conflict of interest are reassigned to maintain impartiality and are subject to additional oversight.
:::

:::details Management Vote of Removal — Appeal Process
Staff removed by a Management vote are entitled to an **OSC Procedural Review** to verify due process was followed.
:::

:::details Staff Interaction with Banned Members
Association with banned individuals must be disclosed and may itself be grounds for investigation and removal.
:::

## High Command & Community Manager Investigations

:::warning Mandatory Lead Overseer involvement
Any SID investigation targeting the **Community Manager** requires mandatory **Lead Overseer** involvement at **every stage** — this cannot be waived. Any resulting punishment must receive Lead Overseer approval before execution.
:::

### Lead Investigator Appointment

- **Appointment/Removal:** only the Oversight Committee may appoint or remove Lead Investigators (majority vote + Lead Overseer approval).
- **Position Cap:** maximum **two (2)** Lead Investigators.
- **Eligibility:** clean record required — no prior infractions or moderation history.
- **Management Exclusion:** no Management Division member may hold this role.

:::danger Strict confidentiality
Investigators shall not share, release, discuss, or publicize classified information. A violation of this agreement results in **immediate termination and blacklisting**. This obligation persists after departure from the staff team.

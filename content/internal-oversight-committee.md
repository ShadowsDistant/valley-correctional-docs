## Executive Summary

The **Oversight Committee (OSC)** is the primary regulatory and audit body within Valley Correctional Facility. Its core function is to maintain operational integrity through policy enforcement, community-engagement monitoring, and high-level strategic advisory.

## Core Mandates

1. **Regulatory Auditing** — Conduct routine, unannounced audits of assigned divisions to ensure alignment with bylaws and internal protocols.
2. **Operational Vitality** — Monitor division-wide activity metrics to ensure personnel are actively engaging and maintaining service standards.
3. **Operational Changes** — All major operational changes (handbook amendments, new manager appointments, significant policy shifts) must be reviewed and approved by the committee.

## Operational Protocols

:::details Advisory & Authority Hierarchy
The OSC functions as an **Independent Advisory Body**. Overseers serve as senior consultants, not direct commanders of Division Managers.

If an Overseer identifies policy violations or inactivity, they submit a formal report to the full committee. With majority consensus or Lead Overseer approval, the committee may issue **Directives** that Managers must follow, including corrective actions or removal.

> **⚠️ No Unilateral Action:** Overseers cannot act alone. No single Overseer may independently enact consequences — firing, demoting, or removing individuals requires a formal committee vote.

**Cross-Division Authority Limits:** an Overseer's authority is scoped strictly to their assigned division. Concerns about other divisions must be raised with the Overseer assigned there — or escalated to the Lead Overseer if that seat is vacant.
:::

:::details Independence from Management
- **Separation of Powers:** no Management Division member may hold an OSC seat.
- **Non-Interference:** Management holds no authority over OSC internal proceedings.
- **External Review:** the OSC may review and vote on concerns regarding any manager, including the Community Manager.
:::

:::details Appointment & Rank Status
- **Eligibility:** membership is limited to former Managers, hand-picked by the Lead Overseer based on divisional expertise.
- **Rank Equivalence:** Overseers operate outside the standard staff hierarchy — ranking above all general, mid-level, and divisional leadership, but below the Community Manager.
- **Exemptions:** Overseers are exempt from divisional quotas and cannot be removed from a division by its Management; removal requires Lead Overseer authorization.
- **Removal:** committee members may only be removed by majority committee vote with Lead Overseer approval. Managers can recommend removal but cannot enact it.
:::

:::details Voting Protocols
- **Division-Specific Votes:** only the Overseer(s) assigned to that division and the Lead Overseer vote.
- **Full Committee Votes:** facility-wide matters require the full committee (e.g. committee member removal, SID Lead Investigator appointment, major policy changes).
- **Tie-Breaking:** the Lead Overseer casts the deciding vote.
:::

:::details OSC Vacancy Contingency
Vacant OSC seats must be filled within **60 days**. If a seat remains vacant past this period, the Lead Overseer must document the reason and establish a coverage plan using existing Overseers.
:::

## Oversight Hearings

:::note Hearing Protocols
**Frequency:** weekly — scheduled by the Lead Overseer. · **Attendance:** open to summoned division members and management; Overseer attendance is optional unless mandated. · **Speaking Time:** 5 minutes per Committee Member; the Lead Overseer may extend as needed.
:::

### Summons & Compliance

Attendance at a Committee hearing is a direct administrative order.

- **Non-Cooperation:** failure to attend or cooperate may result in administrative action up to termination, subject to Lead Overseer approval.
- **Integrity Clause:** all testifying members are under an **Obligation of Truth**. Lying, omitting, or hiding behind unauthorized redactions is a fireable offense.
- **Information Access:** redactions and classifications do not apply within OSC hearings unless specifically authorized by the Lead Overseer.

## OSC Moderation Powers

:::note Moderation Authority
Overseers are granted moderation permissions **exclusively during active shifts** when operating in an official oversight capacity.

- **Enforcement:** Overseers may enforce facility rules and issue punishments per established guidelines.
- **Roleplay Restriction:** Overseers may **not** issue roleplay commands.
- **Primary Objective:** supervising Management and high-ranking officials — moderation is secondary.
- **Scope Limitation:** the OSC is not a replacement for standard moderation staff.
:::

## Division Membership Cap

Maximum **two (2) Overseers** per division at any time. Vacancy management is the Lead Overseer's responsibility.

## SID Lead Investigator Appointment

- **Appointment Authority:** only the OSC may appoint Lead Investigators.
- **Removal Authority:** the OSC may remove at any time, subject to majority vote and Lead Overseer approval.
- **Position Cap:** maximum **two (2) Lead Investigators**.
- **Eligibility:** clean record required — no prior infractions or moderation history.
- **Management Exclusion:** no Management Division member may hold this role.
